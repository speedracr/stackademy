About us

Hi there!

I am Jonas, the founder and one of the people behind Stackademy.

With Stackademy, we're bringing the intense, full-time programming course to Berlin that we ourselves would want to take part in:
Rather than working through countless online courses with varying degrees of success, only to find out you don't end up where you intended to go, Stackademy offers you the chance to dedicate 12 weeks to learn full-time how to plan, develop and deploy web applications in the conducive environment you're looking for.

Once you finish the course, you are ready to take your new knowledge to the next level by hashing out your own ideas, starting to work on projects or moving into a tech-focused position within in a company or startup - in short: you will have become a builder.

If that is for you, then join us for the ride and apply.

Curious to get the full story? Read on.

I don't know about you, but it didn't take Marc Andreessen's proclamation that "software is eating the world" for me to get interested in the power of code. In fact, if computer science classes in my high school had been more accessible, I might have gotten started a lot earlier.

Chances are, though, that your experience is similar to mine: At school, it's the ten nerds of the year group who take computer science, with all of them knowing more than the teacher already.
So unless you stumble into self-teaching early on or decide to study a multi-year computer science course at university, you're left with picking from dozens of online courses or joining twice-a-week evening classes, maybe even about PHP, as your options for learning to program.

When I quit my job to dedicate a larger chunk of time to teaching myself to code, I did go for the online course option and was mostly frustrated by seeing how little of what I was learning ever stuck. And course projects like calculator applications and Guess-the-number games didn't help, either.


However, I did manage to get deep enough into HTML and CSS to be able to put webpages together, and I had heard about programming courses taking off in the U.S. that were actually made from the ground up to teach modern web application development.
With my own experience far from ideal, this course model actually made a lot of sense to me - you can probably recall a rewarding learning experience that came only through dedication and will power.

Having lived in Berlin for four years by this point, I was lucky enough to have met the right people to contact and question about the potential and ideal setup for such a course, and teamed up with Daniel, who I knew from university, to make Stackademy a reality. With support lined up beyond the course, we progressed to bring exciting guest lectures on board and get sound advice regarding our curriculum.

Now it's time to get real and have a great first course in one of Europe's leading startup cities. I look forward to seeing you here!

- jonas
